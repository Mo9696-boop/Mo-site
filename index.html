Projects & Initiatives

Three things I built because they didn't exist when I needed them.

🔬
CVLab
Career Mentoring

Most researchers are more capable than their CVs suggest - they just don't have the language to say it. CVLab started as a side project to help a few friends. It became something bigger: 100+ researchers mentored, and a growing community of scientists figuring out what comes next.

📡
La Valorisation
Newsletter - French

Nobody taught me what a SATT was during my PhD. Or what a déclaration d'invention actually triggers. Or how France 2030 money reaches a lab. La Valorisation exists because that information shouldn't be reserved for insiders - it should reach every researcher, early enough to matter.

🚀
Academia to Industry
Newsletter - English

The hardest part of leaving academia isn't finding a job - it's letting go of the belief that your PhD is only useful in a lab. This newsletter is for researchers ready to think differently about what they've built and where it can take them.

Background

Who I Am

I did my PhD in oncology. Cancer research. The kind of field where the distance between a discovery and a patient who needs it can feel unbridgeable. That gap convinced me early that the most capable science is not the science that gets published - it's the science that leaves the lab. Everything I build is rooted in that belief: research has an obligation to create value beyond the page.
